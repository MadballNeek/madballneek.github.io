Public Alpha - December 2014

Introduction

Firstly, thank you for your interest in participating in the first public alpha for Demons with Shotguns!

If you have any questions about anything, send us an email at nickdimucci@mindshaftgames.com.

The focus of this alpha is to receive feedback on all currently implemented game modes and Cemetery level design. Of course, we’re also looking for any and all feedback on any aspects of the game that you may have.

There are some basic minimum requirements to play Demons with Shotguns:

  • At least one other person to play with
  • This is a local multiplayer only game for 2-4 players
  • One player can use the keyboard, all other players must be using a supported controller
  • Xbox 360 controller recommended
  • List of all supported controllers can be found here
  • A PC with Windows OS (preferably)
  • See the FAQ below if you’re on Mac or Linux

Providing Feedback & Bug Reports

In the game, there is a very small, transparent, button in the lower right corner labeled “Bug”.

When clicked, it will bring up a window.

You can use this form to send us a bug report. Try to be as detailed as possible. Include what game mode and level you were playing, how many players, etc.

You can also use this form to send us any general feedback you may have about the game (or again, you can email us directly). Just make sure to change the type to “Feedback”. When providing feedback, don’t hold back! Give it to us straight :)

However, we do have a specific feedback form we would like for you to fill out, which we will email to you at the end of the alpha. It shouldn’t take you longer than a few minutes to complete.

One quick note, we know the menus are a bit janky at the moment. While they are functional, redoing them is on the list, so bare with us for the moment :)

Game Rules

Game Modes

Demons with Shotguns features multiple game modes to choose from. Here are the included game modes in this alpha, with a quick summary of the rules:

  • Deathmatch
  • Free-for-all. Frag all other players. First player to 25 frags within 5 minutes wins.
  • Team Deathmatch
  • Team based variant of Deathmatch. Players on a team share the same number of frags.
  • Soul Reaping
  • Free-for-all. All players start with 5 soul energy. When you frag a player, they will lose a soul energy, releasing it into the game for anyone to collect. First person to obtain 10 soul energy within 5 minutes wins. Be forewarned, as you cannot respawn when you’ve been drained of all of your soul energy!
  • Team Soul Reaping
  • Team based variant of Soul Reaping. Players on a team share from the same pool of soul energy.
  • Capture the Soul
  • Free-for-all variant of capture the flag. When a player is fragged, their soul is released into the game. Players must fight to maintain control of the soul and return it back to the capture base for a point. Only one soul can be active within the game at any time. First player to 5 captures within 5 minutes wins.
  • Team Capture the Soul
  • Team based variant of Capture the Soul. Players on a team share the same number of captures.
  • Last Soul Standing
  • Free-for-all. Round based version of Deathmatch where each player only has one life and cannot respawn. The last player standing wins the round. First player to win 10 rounds wins.

For team based modes, you can turn on/off friendly fire in the Options menu.

Tarot Cards

Demons with Shotguns features randomly dropped tarot cards. These tarot cards can be either a power-up, or a power-down, so pick them up at your own risk!

Abilities

There are three forms of jumping.

  • Regular Jump (performed by just holding down the Jump button)
  • Double Jump (performed by double tapping the Jump button at the apex of a jump)
  • Shotgun Jump (performed by aiming your shotgun down and shooting. Think rocket jumping)

Players have at their disposal three items.

Your shotgun, which is your main attack. You can aim it in 4 directions (up, down, left, right). It has limited ammo (up to 6 shells). There are ammo pickups available throughout the level.

Your one-time-use holy water grenade (come on, you didn’t know holy water was explosive?). The grenade will explode upon touching another player (or being shot at), or after a short timer.

Your shield which can be used to block shots and deflect them back (use wisely, as the shield has limited use before being destroyed). You cannot move when shielded.

Players can also perform a melee attack, which requires you to charge it up. The longer you charge, the more powerful the attack. When released, you’ll perform a brutal shield dash. However, if you fail to hit anyone, you’ll be stunned momentarily, unable to move. So use your melee attack wisely!

Players can also dash, helping to close the gap between opponents, or be used to escape!

Analytics

For the sake of complete transparency, the alpha build of Demons with Shotguns will be sending completely anonymous analytics data while you play. This is completely normal for almost any game (many of which don’t even let you know they’re doing it!).

Some of the type of data that’ll be sent is

  • Game mode you’re playing
  • Number of players, and their input devices
  • Basic system specs (CPU, GPU, OS)
  • Memory usage of the game
  • Basic game events such as frags

This data will help us understand player behaviour and balance game modes greatly, as well as capture any errors or exceptions that may occur.
